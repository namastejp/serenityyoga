Easy Yoga
at Home

Simple, calming yoga poses you can practice anywhere — no equipment needed. Perfect for beginners and all levels.

20 Essential Yoga Poses

Step-by-step guidance for each pose — start your wellness journey today

Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

🟢 Beginner

Stand tall with feet together, arms at sides, spine elongated. Breathe deeply and feel grounded. This is the foundation of all standing poses. Keep your shoulders relaxed and gaze forward. Hold for 5–10 deep breaths.

✨ Improves posture and body awareness

Child's Pose (Balasana)

🟢 Beginner

Kneel and sit back on heels, stretch arms forward on the mat, forehead resting gently down. Hold for 5–10 breaths. A deeply restorative resting pose you can return to anytime during practice.

✨ Relieves back tension and calms the mind

Downward Dog (Adho Mukha)

🟢 Beginner

From hands and knees, lift hips up and back forming an inverted V-shape. Press palms firmly into mat, try to press heels toward the floor. Keep arms straight. Hold for 5 deep breaths.

✨ Stretches spine, shoulders and hamstrings

Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

🟢 Beginner

Stand on one foot, place the other foot on your inner thigh or calf (not on the knee). Bring hands together at chest. Focus on a fixed point ahead. Hold for 30 seconds each side.

✨ Builds balance and concentration

Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

🟢 Beginner

Step one foot forward into a deep lunge. Raise both arms overhead, palms facing each other. Square hips forward toward front foot. Back foot is at 45°. Hold for 5 breaths and switch sides.

✨ Strengthens legs, opens chest and hips

Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

🟢 Beginner

From a wide stance, turn right foot out and bend right knee. Extend both arms wide parallel to the floor. Gaze over your front hand. Hips and shoulders face the side. Hold 5 breaths each side.

✨ Builds leg strength and stamina

Cat-Cow Pose (Marjaryasana)

🟢 Beginner

On hands and knees, alternate arching and rounding your back with each breath. Inhale for cow pose (belly drops, head rises). Exhale for cat pose (spine rounds up). Repeat 10 slow cycles.

✨ Warms up the spine and relieves back pain

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

🟢 Beginner

Lie face down on mat. Place hands under shoulders with fingers spread. On an inhale, press up lifting chest while keeping hips on the floor. Keep elbows slightly bent. Hold 3–5 breaths.

✨ Strengthens back muscles, opens chest

Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)

🟢 Beginner

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on floor, hip-width apart. Press feet firmly into the floor and lift hips toward the ceiling. Optionally interlace fingers under your back. Hold 5 breaths.

✨ Strengthens glutes, thighs and lower back

Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana)

🟢 Beginner

Sit with both legs straight out in front. Inhale and lengthen your spine tall, then exhale and slowly fold forward reaching toward your feet. Focus on a flat back rather than rounding. Hold 10 breaths.

✨ Stretches hamstrings and calms nervous system

Seated Twist (Ardha Matsyendrasana)

🟢 Beginner

Sit with legs extended. Bend right knee and cross right foot outside left thigh. Twist your torso to the right, using left elbow on right knee as leverage. Sit tall. Hold 5 breaths each side.

✨ Detoxifies organs and improves spinal flexibility

Lion's Breath Pose (Simhasana)

🟢 Beginner

Sit comfortably in a kneeling or cross-legged position. Take a deep inhale through your nose, then exhale forcefully through your open mouth with tongue sticking out and eyes wide open. Repeat 5 times.

✨ Releases facial tension and relieves stress

Legs Up the Wall (Viparita Karani)

🟢 Beginner

Lie on your back close to a wall and extend legs straight up resting against the wall. Arms rest by your sides with palms facing up. Close your eyes and breathe slowly. Stay for 5–10 minutes. Perfect before sleep.

✨ Reduces fatigue and improves circulation

Butterfly Pose (Baddha Konasana)

🟢 Beginner

Sit tall with the soles of your feet pressed together and let your knees drop out to the sides. Hold your feet with both hands. Gently press your knees toward the floor. Hold for 10 slow breaths.

✨ Opens hips and stretches inner thighs

Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)

🟡 Intermediate

Stand with feet wide apart. Turn right foot out 90°. Reach right hand toward right shin or the floor while extending left arm straight up. Stack hips and open chest to the side. Hold 5 breaths each side.

✨ Stretches sides of body and improves balance

Pigeon Pose (Eka Pada Rajakapotasana)

🟡 Intermediate

From downward dog, bring right knee forward behind right wrist. Extend left leg straight back on the mat. Lower your body down resting on forearms or flat on the mat. Hold for 10 deep breaths each side.

✨ Deep hip opener, releases stored tension

Boat Pose (Navasana)

🟡 Intermediate

Sit on the floor with knees bent. Lean back slightly keeping spine straight and lift your feet so shins are parallel to the floor. Extend arms forward. Engage your core strongly. Hold for 5–10 breaths.

✨ Strengthens core muscles and hip flexors

Camel Pose (Ustrasana)

🟡 Intermediate

Kneel with hips directly over knees. Reach back with both hands to hold your heels. Push your hips forward and lift your chest up toward the ceiling. Let your head drop back gently. Hold 5 breaths.

✨ Opens chest, stretches shoulders powerfully

Supine Twist (Supta Jathara Parivartanasana)

🟢 Beginner

Lie flat on your back. Draw both knees into your chest, then gently let them fall to one side while spreading your arms wide. Keep both shoulders on the mat. Hold 10 breaths each side. Perfect before sleep.

✨ Releases lower back tension and improves digestion

Corpse Pose (Savasana)

🟢 Beginner

Lie completely flat on your back. Let arms and legs fall slightly apart, palms face up, eyes gently closed. Release all effort and tension. Focus only on the natural flow of your breath. Stay for 5–15 minutes. This is the most important pose in yoga — it allows your body to fully absorb the benefits of your practice.

✨ Deep relaxation, integrates all practice benefits

🌿 Tips for Home Practice

🕰 Best Time

Morning practice energizes your day. Evening practice helps you unwind and sleep better.

🧺 What You Need

A yoga mat or soft carpet is enough. Wear comfortable, stretchy clothing.

💧 Hydration

Drink water before and after practice. Avoid eating a heavy meal 1–2 hours before yoga.

🫁 Breathing

Always breathe through your nose. Never hold your breath during a pose. Breath guides movement.
